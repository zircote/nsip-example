## Trait Glossary

- **BWT** (Birth Weight): Weight at birth in lbs. **Lower is better** — reduces lambing difficulties.
- **WWT** (Weaning Weight): Growth to ~60 days in lbs. **Higher is better** — faster early growth.
- **PWWT** (Post-Weaning Weight): Growth after weaning in lbs. **Higher is better**.
- **YWT** (Yearling Weight): Weight at ~12 months in lbs. **Higher is better** — key for market lamb production.
- **NLB** (Number of Lambs Born): Fertility trait — lambs per lambing. **Higher is better**.
- **US Index**: NSIP US Hair Sheep index — composite genetic merit score for hair sheep breeds.
- **SRC Index**: Sheep Recording and Classing index — composite score including maternal traits.

**Accuracy** indicates EBV reliability:
- < 40%: Low (limited data)
- 40–60%: Moderate (own performance + some relatives)
- 60–80%: High (good progeny data)
- > 80%: Very high (extensive progeny testing)

**Predicted Offspring EBVs** are midparent values: `(sire_EBV + dam_EBV) / 2`
